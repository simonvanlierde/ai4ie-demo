## What works today, per field

The one-paragraph honest versions. Papers behind every claim are in the
[literature list](../literature/), pre-filtered by the links below.

### LCA & footprinting

Two things demonstrably work: **tabular ML** for rapid impact screening and inventory
gap-filling (validated repeatedly since 2017), and **LLM extraction** of inventory
data from literature and reports (working systems at F1 ≈ 0.82–0.86, always with
human verification). The field's consensus rule is worth tattooing somewhere:
*extraction, not generation* — a 2025 benchmark found 37% of general-purpose LLM
answers to LCA questions inaccurate or misleading, with hallucinated-citation rates
up to 40%. Automated footprinting deploys at catalog scale for common products and
degrades off it. [Papers →](../literature/?ie=lca)

### MFA & stocks and flows

The most mature corner of the map. Satellite-based material stock mapping works at
**national scale (10 m)** from free data, and a **global estimate (90 m, 547 Gt in
buildings)** exists — with an honest validation spread of 53–200% against independent
studies, which is the real accuracy ceiling. Night-lights proxies and deep multi-source
fusion improve on single sources but still need ground data. Street-view CV for
building attributes is demonstrated feasibility: good in the training city, sharply
worse elsewhere. [Papers →](../literature/?ie=mfa)

### Waste, EoL & circularity

The most *published* corner. Task-tuned detection and counting of waste and
components is well-validated; camera-based flow characterization runs in operating
WEEE sorting plants; label-OCR identification is deployed in pilots (and collapses
from 96% to 40% readability between repair shop and recycler — condition at EoL is
the recurring villain). Foundation models (SAM, CLIP) demo impressively but need a
class-assignment layer and sorting-grade evaluation before they count as methods.
Robotic disassembly works for narrow product families.
[Papers →](../literature/?ie=eol) · Full depth in the
[CV taxonomy worked example](cv-taxonomy/).

### Not mapped yet

Input–output analysis, forecasting, and scenario modeling have no vetted entries —
our research sweep found no claims in these areas that survived verification, which
is itself a data point. If you work there and know better, this is the easiest place
to contribute.
