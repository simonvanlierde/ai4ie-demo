dimensions**: the IE field (LCA, MFA, waste & circularity, …) and the AI technique
(LLMs, computer vision, tabular ML, …). Every entry links to the
[literature list](/literature/) pre-filtered to the same tags, so the map doubles as
an index into the reading list.

## What works today, per field

One paragraph per field on what actually works. The papers behind every claim are in
the [literature list](/literature/), pre-filtered by the links below.

### LCA & footprinting

Two things demonstrably work: **tabular ML** for rapid impact screening and inventory
gap-filling (validated repeatedly since 2017), and **LLM extraction** of inventory
data from literature and reports (working systems at F1 ≈ 0.82–0.86 <Cite id="kumarLargeLanguageModelbased2025" />, always with
human verification). The consensus rule across this work: use LLMs to *extract*
inventory data, never to generate it <Cite id="preussLargeLanguageModels2024" />. A 2025 benchmark found 37% of general-purpose
LLM answers to LCA questions inaccurate or misleading, with hallucinated-citation
rates up to 40% <Cite id="donaldsonExpertGroundedBenchmark2025" />. Automated footprinting deploys at catalog scale for common products <Cite id="balajiFlamingoEnvironmentalImpact2023" />
and degrades off it. [Papers →](/literature/?ie=lca)

### MFA & stocks and flows

The most mature corner of the map. Satellite-based material stock mapping works at
**national scale (10 m)** <Cite id="haberlHighResolutionMaps2021" /> from free data, and a **global estimate (90 m, 547 Gt in
buildings)** <Cite id="haberlGlobalBuiltEnvironment2024" /> exists. Mind the validation spread, though: 53–200% against independent
studies, and that is the real accuracy ceiling. Night-lights proxies <Cite id="peledSpatialMaterialStock2021" /> and deep multi-source
fusion <Cite id="baoDeepLearningFusion2023" /> improve on single sources but still need ground data. Street-view CV for
building attributes is demonstrated feasibility: good in the training city, sharply
worse elsewhere <Cite id="huangImageBasedPrediction2024" />. [Papers →](/literature/?ie=mfa)

### Waste, EoL & circularity

The corner with the most publications. Task-tuned detection and counting of waste and
components is well-validated <Cite id="majchrowskaDeepLearningbasedWaste2022" />; camera-based flow characterization runs in operating
WEEE sorting plants <Cite id="vogelgesangAutomatedMaterialFlow2025" />; label-OCR identification is deployed in pilots, where readability
collapses from 96% in repair shops to 40% at the recycler <Cite id="sterkensProductLabelIdentification2023" />. Worn, dirty, damaged
products are the recurring failure mode. Foundation models (<Cite id="kirillovSegmentAnything2023">SAM</Cite>, <Cite id="radfordLearningTransferableVisual2021">CLIP</Cite>) demo
impressively but need a class-assignment layer and sorting-grade evaluation before
they count as methods.
Robotic disassembly works for narrow product families <Cite id="ameurFutureRoboticDisassembly2025" />.
[Papers →](/literature/?ie=eol) · Full depth in the
[CV taxonomy worked example](/applications-map/cv-taxonomy/).

### Not mapped yet

The blank space in this map is worth as much as the filled cells, so here it is named
rather than left implicit. Two kinds of gap: **IE fields** with no vetted entries, and
**AI techniques** with no tag in the vocabulary yet.

One caveat on how to read this. None of it claims the work doesn't exist. It claims we
have not vetted any of it — our sweep either found nothing that survived verification,
or never looked. Each line is the easiest possible contribution: one paper, one entry.

#### AI techniques with no tag

Tags are added together with their first entry, so these stay out of the vocabulary until
someone brings a paper that earns one.

- `TODO` **Uncertainty quantification & Bayesian methods.** Uncertainty is this discipline's
  central methodological concern and the map has no column for it. Conformal prediction included.
- `TODO` **Graph neural networks & network analysis.** Supply chains, IO tables, material-flow
  networks, molecular graphs: most IE data is a network, and nothing here treats it as one.
- `TODO` **Surrogate models & emulators.** Including physics-informed. This is how scenario
  work actually gets done with ML, rather than in principle.
- `TODO` **Causal inference.** Rebound effects and policy attribution are causal claims,
  usually made with correlational tools.
- `TODO` **Reinforcement learning.** Disassembly sequence planning and process control. The
  EoL paragraph above already discusses robotic disassembly with no technique tag behind it.
- `TODO` **Knowledge graphs & ontologies.** LCI interoperability and semantic flow matching,
  distinct from NLP extraction.
- `TODO` **Recommender & matching systems.** The method behind industrial symbiosis, and
  behind matching products to emission factors.
- `TODO` **Time-series & forecasting.** Distinct from tabular ML.
- `TODO` **Federated & privacy-preserving learning.** [Local AI & hardware](/tools/local-ai/)
  argues for keeping sensitive data in-house; this is how institutions learn from data they
  are never going to pool.
- `TODO` **Explainability.** Whether a model-based assessment claim can be interrogated.

#### Where the gaps meet

Several of these are not separate holes but one cell each: **chemicals × graph neural
networks**, **scenarios × surrogates**, **symbiosis × matching**, **EoL × reinforcement
learning**. Those four are the most concrete places to start, because each names both a
field and a method rather than a vague area.
