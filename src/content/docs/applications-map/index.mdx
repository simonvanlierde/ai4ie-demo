## What works today, per field

One paragraph per field on what actually works. The papers behind every claim are in
the [literature list](../literature/), pre-filtered by the links below.

### LCA & footprinting

Two things demonstrably work: **tabular ML** for rapid impact screening and inventory
gap-filling (validated repeatedly since 2017), and **LLM extraction** of inventory
data from literature and reports (working systems at F1 ≈ 0.82–0.86, always with
human verification). The consensus rule across this work: use LLMs to *extract*
inventory data, never to generate it. A 2025 benchmark found 37% of general-purpose
LLM answers to LCA questions inaccurate or misleading, with hallucinated-citation
rates up to 40%. Automated footprinting deploys at catalog scale for common products
and degrades off it. [Papers →](../literature/?ie=lca)

### MFA & stocks and flows

The most mature corner of the map. Satellite-based material stock mapping works at
**national scale (10 m)** from free data, and a **global estimate (90 m, 547 Gt in
buildings)** exists. Mind the validation spread, though: 53–200% against independent
studies, and that is the real accuracy ceiling. Night-lights proxies and deep multi-source
fusion improve on single sources but still need ground data. Street-view CV for
building attributes is demonstrated feasibility: good in the training city, sharply
worse elsewhere. [Papers →](../literature/?ie=mfa)

### Waste, EoL & circularity

The corner with the most publications. Task-tuned detection and counting of waste and
components is well-validated; camera-based flow characterization runs in operating
WEEE sorting plants; label-OCR identification is deployed in pilots, where readability
collapses from 96% in repair shops to 40% at the recycler. Worn, dirty, damaged
products are the recurring failure mode. Foundation models (SAM, CLIP) demo
impressively but need a class-assignment layer and sorting-grade evaluation before
they count as methods.
Robotic disassembly works for narrow product families.
[Papers →](../literature/?ie=eol) · Full depth in the
[CV taxonomy worked example](../applications-map/cv-taxonomy/).
