A community-curated guide to using AI in industrial ecology (IE), maintained by the
**AI4IE working group**.

:::note[A seeded proposal — argue with it]
Working-group members seeded every section with real, checkable entries, and all of
it is open to challenge. If you disagree with a maturity call or miss your subfield,
that's a [pull request](https://github.com/simonvanlierde/ai4ie-demo/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md).
:::

## How it fits together

One tag vocabulary connects the whole site. For every application area you can
follow the same three steps:

1. **See what works today** on the [map of AI applications](applications-map/) —
   e.g. computer vision at end-of-life;
2. **Read the evidence** in the [literature list](literature/), pre-filtered to the
   same tags — e.g. [EoL × computer vision](literature/?ie=eol&ml=cv);
3. **Try it** on the [demos page](demos/) where a demo exists — e.g. zero-shot
   recognition running in your browser.

## Sections

| # | Section | What's in it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Map of AI applications in IE](applications-map/) | What works today, by IE field × AI technique, with honest maturity calls. |
| 2 | [Literature & resources](literature/) | A tag-filterable reading list: AI in IE, research practice, and ML landmarks. |
| 3 | [Best practices & guides](best-practices/) | Getting started, pitfalls, verification checklists, reusable prompts, Claude skills. |
| 4 | [Tools & hardware](tools/) | Models, apps, libraries — and what to run them on, including local options for sensitive data. |
| 5 | [Demos](demos/) | In-browser and embedded demos, each tied to a cell of the map. |

## Contributing

Add a paper, a tool, an application, or a demo by opening a pull request — data
lives in two YAML files and every page has an edit pencil. The
[contributing guide](https://github.com/simonvanlierde/ai4ie-demo/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md)
explains the format and where each type of entry goes.
