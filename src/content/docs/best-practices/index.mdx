Two different things get called "using AI". One is **AI as a research method**: a
model inside your pipeline, producing results you publish. The other is **AI as an
assistant**: a chatbot or coding agent that helps you write the code and the paper.
They fail in different ways and need different safeguards.

One rule covers both: **if you couldn't tell a good output from a bad one, don't
delegate it.**

## AI as a research method

Using ML, deep learning, or generative models to process or produce data you will
publish. Coding assistants have lowered the barrier to running a model much further
than the barrier to noticing a broken one.

- **Start with a baseline.** Report a simple model — linear regression will do —
  alongside any complex one. If the gap is small, the complex model needs a reason
  to exist, and sometimes the honest answer is that the question didn't need ML.
- **Isolate the test set first.** Data leakage is the single most common
  reproducibility failure in IE ML work, and normalization, gap-filling, or feature
  selection performed before the split is the usual route in. Time-structured data
  needs a time-aware split, not a random one. The
  [ML-reproducibility checklist](/best-practices/checklists/#machine-learning-reproducibility)
  is the full version; the
  [leakage self-check prompt](/best-practices/prompts-and-skills/#data-leakage-self-check)
  audits a pipeline you've already built.
- **Expect performance to collapse off the training distribution.** It happens
  repeatedly across the map: street-view models degrade sharply outside their
  training city, and label OCR falls from 96% in repair shops to 40% at the recycler
  ([evidence](/applications-map/#waste-eol--circularity)). Report where your model
  was validated, and don't claim beyond it.
- **Validate against something independent.** Satellite-derived material stocks
  land within 53–200% of independent studies
  ([evidence](/applications-map/#mfa--stocks-and-flows)). That spread is the
  accuracy ceiling, so it belongs in your results rather than an appendix.
- **Use generative models to extract, not to generate.** The consensus in LCA work
  is that LLMs can pull inventory data out of documents with human verification, and
  should not produce inventory values themselves
  ([evidence](/applications-map/#lca--footprinting)). The
  [extraction prompt](/best-practices/prompts-and-skills/#extract-without-inventing)
  encodes the difference. Synthetic data is a modeling assumption; if you use it,
  it goes in the methods section.
- **Report the pipeline in order.** "We split, then normalized, then trained" tells a
  reader more than the architecture diagram does. Pin versions, seeds, and the
  environment; state the reason when data or code is withheld.
- **Carry uncertainty through.** A model's point estimate is no more certain than
  one from a mass balance. Uncertainty is IE's central methodological concern and
  still has no column in the [map](/applications-map/#ai-techniques-with-no-tag).

## AI as an assistant

Chatbots and coding agents helping you write code, prose, or analysis. The model is
fast and has no stake in being right, so keep the loop short enough that mistakes
surface while you can still catch them.

- **Good at transforming what you give it:** summarizing, restructuring,
  translating, explaining, drafting, and writing code you can test. You can check
  any of those faster than you could do them yourself. It is bad at being a source;
  see [pitfalls](#pitfalls) for the measured failure rates.
- **Give it the real context.** Most "the AI is bad at this" complaints trace to a
  missing instruction: the actual data schema, the units, the method's constraints,
  the paper's argument. Paste the thing rather than describing it.
- **Small diffs you actually read.** Accept changes you can review in one sitting,
  with tests that would catch a wrong answer and a known-answer check for anything
  numerical. The [coding checklist](/best-practices/checklists/#ai-assisted-coding)
  is the concrete version.
- **Review in a fresh session.** A model grading its own work is a poor reviewer.
  The [adversarial review prompt](/best-practices/prompts-and-skills/#adversarial-review-of-ai-written-code)
  starts a skeptical second pass with no memory of having written the code.
- **Verify before citing, every time.** Ask for the primary source and open it. The
  [verification prompt](/best-practices/prompts-and-skills/#verify-a-claim-before-citing-it)
  is built to fail loudly instead of substituting a plausible paper.
- **Package what you repeat.** A workflow you run more than twice belongs in a
  [skill file](/best-practices/prompts-and-skills/#claude-skill-files) rather than in
  your memory of how you phrased it last time.
- **Learn prompting properly** from the [Prompt Engineering Guide](https://www.promptingguide.ai),
  which is free and tool-agnostic.

## Pitfalls

Where people get burned, each with a documented case. The first two apply to both
tracks; the third decides whether you can use a hosted service at all.

- **Hallucinated citations.** In a controlled study, 55% of the references
  GPT-3.5 produced and 18% of GPT-4's were entirely fabricated, and much of the
  remainder had substantive errors
  ([Walters & Wilder, 2023](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-41032-5)). Newer
  models are better but not safe: US lawyers were sanctioned for filing a brief
  with six invented cases
  ([Mata v. Avianca](https://apnews.com/article/artificial-intelligence-chatgpt-fake-case-lawyers-d6ae9fa79d0542db9e1455397aef381c)).
  Rule: a citation an AI gives you does not exist until you've opened it.
- **Confident-but-wrong numbers.** Models state numbers with the same fluency
  whether recalled correctly, approximated, or invented; there is no tonal
  difference to detect. Any number destined for a paper gets traced to a primary
  source or recomputed.
- **Leaking confidential data.** Anything pasted into a hosted service leaves your
  machine and may be retained; Samsung banned generative-AI tools after engineers
  pasted internal source code into ChatGPT
  ([TechCrunch, 2023](https://techcrunch.com/2023/05/02/samsung-bans-use-of-generative-ai-tools-like-chatgpt-after-april-internal-data-leak/)).
  For NDA'd inventories and industry data, use the
  [local options on the tools pages](/tools/local-ai/), and check your
  data-management plan before pasting.
